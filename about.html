About Me

A short reflective page that captures who I am, what I’m working toward, and how I’m growing through this journey.

Bio

I’m an aspiring front-end engineer who started learning software development a little over a year ago. My journey began with curiosity and has grown into a genuine passion for building clean, functional, and user-friendly web interfaces. I enjoy learning new technologies, experimenting with layouts, and seeing my progress come to life through projects. Right now, I’m focused on strengthening my skills in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React, while also exploring the basics of cybersecurity.

Goals in this Program

My main goal is to grow from just understanding concepts to applying them confidently in real projects. I want to complete every task with consistency and clarity — not just to pass, but to truly learn. I hope to become skilled enough to contribute meaningfully to a team, handle front-end challenges independently, and prepare for my first tech role. Most importantly, I want to develop the discipline and structure that will help me keep improving long after this program ends.

Areas of Low Confidence

Sometimes I struggle with self-doubt, especially when I see others making faster progress. I also feel less confident when it comes to backend topics, advanced JavaScript patterns, or explaining technical ideas on the spot. But I’m learning that growth takes time — and that consistency is more powerful than comparison. My plan is to keep facing those weak spots one at a time until they become strengths.

Note to Future Self

Dear Future Me, if you’re reading this, I hope you’ve seen how far you’ve come. Remember that every night you spent learning and every moment you pushed through doubt was worth it. Keep that same hunger to learn, stay humble, and never forget why you started. You don’t need to have everything figured out — just keep building, keep growing, and stay close to your purpose.

Extra Thoughts

This journey has shown me that real progress is built on small, steady steps. Some days I feel behind, but those are the days that teach me the most about patience and persistence. I’ve also realised that sharing knowledge and connecting with others in tech not only helps me grow faster but makes the journey less lonely. My goal is to always keep that collaborative spirit, even when things get tough.
